Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our dental services and procedures.

How often should I visit the dentist?
Most patients should visit the dentist every six months for a check-up and cleaning. However, some individuals with specific dental conditions may need more frequent visits. Your dentist will recommend the appropriate schedule based on your oral health needs.
What should I do in a dental emergency?
If you're experiencing a dental emergency such as severe pain, a knocked-out tooth, or bleeding, call our emergency line immediately. For a knocked-out tooth, keep it moist (preferably in milk) and bring it with you. For pain or swelling, rinse with warm salt water and take over-the-counter pain relievers if appropriate.
How can I improve my smile?
We offer various cosmetic dentistry options to enhance your smile, including teeth whitening, veneers, bonding, and orthodontic treatments. During a consultation, our dentists will evaluate your smile and recommend the most appropriate treatments to achieve your desired results.
Are dental X-rays safe?
Yes, dental X-rays are safe. We use digital X-ray technology that significantly reduces radiation exposure compared to traditional film X-rays. Additionally, we provide protective equipment and only take X-rays when necessary for diagnosis and treatment planning.
What payment options do you offer?
We accept most major insurance plans, credit cards, cash, and checks. We also offer financing options through CareCredit and in-house payment plans for certain treatments. Our administrative team will work with you to find the most suitable payment option for your needs.
